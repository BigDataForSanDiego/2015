This application allows you to automatically be notified if a disaster occurs near your loved ones.

 

It's simple to use, just create an account!

 

Once you add your contacts, you can monitor each others' locations and find out when a disaster happens near one another. 

 

This application also provides information from the American Red Cross for you to use in case of different disasters.

This map view shows each of your contacts with a 3 mile buffer around their locations. The buffer acts as a detection zone so when a disaster occurs within the buffer zone, you will be automatically contacted and notified. 

 

The datasets used are from San Diego County (fire perimeter, evacuation notifications, and localized hazards), Interior Geospatial Emergency Management System (warnings and severe), ArcGIS Live Feed (NOAA's short term warnings, earthquakes, and hurricanes), and the National Weather Services (using GeoViewer's ArcGIS web service).

 

The refresh intervals are set to 3 minutes for each dataset, following the refresh frequency of NOAA's collected data. 

 

Once the "GeoEvent Extension" can be accessed (currently do not have a license), then simple functions can be applied in order to collect real-time data from "contacts" and directly send chats within the app, text messages to their phones, and emails. 

The Red Cross offers a variety of safety and preparedness guides to use in the event of a disaster or other emergency. The user can access this information via this tab.

Community resilience refers to how quickly the state of the community can return to its state before a disaster. Often, it takes an extended period of time for a community to bounce back because relief efforts are spread thin. By helping each other out, this process can be sped up. 

 

This tab shows real-time information on where people in your community need help. If someone has the capability to respond to a neighbor's help request, the two users can communicate via the app or by exchanging contact information. 

 

Datasets from the San Diego County are provided for finding evacuation centers, recovery centers, and evacuation routes. By clicking on one of the centers or an evacuation route, you can easily find directions.

The Operations Dashboard can only be accessed using the "Operations Dashboard" application available for free from Esri's website. 

 

This dashboard provides information on requested help from other users, a chat room for users, and the centers and routes available in the "View Requested Help" tab.
